In your words:

> My humourous take on Rome as a living history, is that of a brand-building project under the
> premise that Rome is a brand that needs work. Scenario: Romulus and Remus have approached my
> branding agency for help to build their new city, and well, Remus is quickly unavailable, and
> there's not a lot of budget to begin with. Under the circumstances, I propose to the client
> what the brand needs and how we can build it. Cut to a few centuries of success later, the
> client is back with a rebranding request, as you'll see in the presentation. The overarching
> theme is of reality — understanding, evaluating, and redefining Rome's reality. At first, the
> reality that Rome is not a city and just a brick wall, is no obstacle. Then, reality becomes a
> battlefield of religion. In the present-day, reality is merely a distraction that we escape by
> traveling to Rome.
