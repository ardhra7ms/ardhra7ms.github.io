Writers in Rome was a month-long study abroad program for a cohort of students from the
University of Washington - Seattle. This creative writing experience was an exploration of
multiple formats of writing, with a focus on poetry. Everyday, we visited historical sites and
museums and had creative, reflective themes to write and experiment with, ranging across
history, art, mysticism, religion, belief systems, cultures, cuisines, and more. I was also
awarded the William P. and Ruth Gerberding Fellowship for this program.

This is a compilation of my writing, art, and photography during the program, in which I explore
poetry, essays, prose poetry, and drawing. The summer program was very experiential and I had a
once in a lifetime experience meeting and conversing with amazing and interesting artists and
warm people, exploring Rome averaging 12k steps a day and counterbalancing it with a tonne of
gelatos.
