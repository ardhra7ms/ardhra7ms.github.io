*Microsoft Purview · Content Designer, sole content owner · ~2024–present.*

The project I've carried the longest: I've been on **DSI from before private preview through
GA and beyond**, across multiple org and team changes. DSI is Purview's first standalone,
pay-as-you-go solution with **AI at its core** — an AI-powered investigation tool for data
security, with a non-linear workflow and a consumption billing model.

## Ramping in (the first four months)
I took content ownership of a space that had been "spinning and stalling" and in invention for
a year. In four months I delivered **end-to-end content across the user journey**, kept
readability below a 10th-grade average, developed research-based terminology, and helped the
product reach **private preview** — including content for demos at multiple levels of review,
up to a potential demo to Satya Nadella.

## Building the system, not the strings
- **Concept map.** I designed a concept map establishing the foundational nouns and verbs of
  the solution (~27 new concepts) — a shared mental model that aligned PM, Engineering, UX, and
  Marketing, and helped new designers assimilate. Colleagues still cite it as the thing that
  oriented them when they joined.
- **Terminology reform.** From three recurring research pain points (unintuitive navigation,
  technical jargon, hidden actions), I led a terminology initiative that became the foundation
  for broader UX fixes: *Vectorize → Prepare data*, *Probe → Examine*, *vector search →
  AI search*, *History → Activities*. I standardized *purge* over *delete*, validated through
  customer ride-alongs.
- **A structure users could hold.** I proposed aligning the experience to the marketing pillars
  (**Identify · Analyze · Mitigate**) to reduce overwhelm and give investigations a spine.

## First impressions: the First Run Experience
For DSI's public preview I designed the **onboarding and First Run Experience** — the first of
its kind for Purview (standalone, pay-as-you-go, AI at its core) — under a 5-day deadline,
through a billing model that kept changing (a new SCU meter on top of Purview PayG). I planned
content for multiple possible billing journeys ahead of time, which is what let us absorb the
changes. Result: a launch with **zero customer incidents** to date, and the FRE became the
model other feature crews now emulate.

**Public preview:** 42 customers completed the first-run experience (**49% conversion**) in the
first month; early partners included Pfizer and Chevron; 16 industries and 17 countries
represented; 76% also using GenAI.

## Fixing wayfinding (the hard retro)
Post-preview, an experience review surfaced **46+ design traps**. A top one: poor wayfinding
and unclear hierarchy — task success at **38%**, with a **58% drop-off** after onboarding. I
led content strategy to refine terminology and IA (page titles, CTAs like *Categorize* and
*Examine*), helped design two new pages (**Summary** and **Activities**) so users could
reorient after interruptions, and numbered the identification steps to set expectations.

## Scaling and growth (GA → beyond)
- **GA at 9.9 readability**; the Credential scan capability at 9.6.
- Simplified **investigation creation from 7 steps to 4**, added search templates and free-trial
  messaging → **tenants with active data usage +14% MoM**.
- Built guardrails for **purge** (a high-risk action, +12% MoM usage): communicating risk,
  clarifying system behavior, validated terminology via ride-alongs.
- Improved cross-product entry (DSPM, Endpoint DLP, Unified Audit Logs) so users move from
  signals to investigations faster.
- Addressed cost transparency (a Forrester gap) through the cost estimator, usage dashboard, and
  FRE billing updates.
