*Microsoft Purview · Content Designer, co-owner of the space · ~2025–present.*

I took on content design for **eDiscovery** alongside DSI — a very different challenge. Where
DSI was being invented, eDiscovery is **mature and legal in nature**, with long-standing
customers and entrenched patterns. The work here was less about envisioning and more about
**strategic infrastructure, carried-over learnings, and measurable usability gains**.

## A content strategy package the PM actually adopted
I built an **eDiscovery content strategy package** on top of existing documentation and my tone
and voice guidance. It proved its value in a post-feature phase of string improvements and bug
fixes: the **PM adopted it directly** to produce UI content to the Content Design team's
standards, then collaborated with me on feedback and iteration. This is the leverage I care
about — a system that scales a voice across people, not one string at a time.

## Usability, driven by real signals
Working from **WBR SEQ signals**, I targeted where customers actually struggled — **case
creation, collections, query building (KQL), and export**:

- Improved **query building and search**.
- **Redesigned export data organization** for usability over readability.
- Carried forward improved design patterns proven in DSI.

**Outcome: ~35% SEQ score improvement** from July 2025 to May 2026. Readability on the March
2026 feature releases improved from **8.6 to 7.9** — hard-won in a dense legal product.

## Trust and AI naming (where I pushed back)
Through an **agent study for eDiscovery**, I showed that **"Posture Agent" wasn't intuitive** —
exposing a gap in how agent categories were defined — and started a Product/Marketing
conversation about it. I challenged business/marketing-approved terminology that didn't fit the
product context, explored alternative naming across agent scenarios, flagged issues like
*"Assign to agent,"* and worked with the agent-coherence team toward clearer, system-oriented
phrasing. I connected this to prior Sentinel Graph work, aligning "Org intelligence" concepts
with the more relatable **"relationship map."**

This is the thread my manager encouraged me to own: **Responsible AI in agent experiences —
transparency, preventing overreliance, and calibrating trust in AI-powered products.**

## Scope
Across **~15 eDiscovery features** (and ~30 in DSI), working with **6 squads**, my impact grew
significantly through the year — now **fully owning the DSI and eDiscovery spaces**.
