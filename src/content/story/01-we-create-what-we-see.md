It's a practice I'm cultivating, much like yoga or spirituality. I'm not perfectly positive everyday, but I'm learning that that isn't the goal anyway. The goal is to arrive at hope at the end of the journey of emotions and experiences, because that's where I find grounding.  I heard this line in a Korean show I love, where one woman describes another as strong like a tree. **Strong not in order to destroy her surroundings, but to embrace them.**

**I want to be known for my optimism.** In a world where being negative in the name of being rational and being a problem solver is a competitive sport, I want to hold on to my optimism whenever possible and provide that as a different option that's possible for all of us.

I sincerely believe that we create what we see. We may not be able to create something beautiful and hopeful, if we can't hope for betterment to begin with.
