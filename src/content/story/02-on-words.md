Engineering is logical and unforgiving. It's rules are for everyone to abide by. That helps in some ways when building systems need so much thinking and process. But, when it comes to corner cases, the systems stop making sense pretty quickly. We have to make exceptions for them all, even the ones you can't consider yet.

Words are always exceptions. They come out of somewhere, combinations from different languages. At some point, a big community decides to use the same word to mean the same thing, to tell stories to each other. That's the basis of humanity, both as trees and as a forest.

The essence of people and humanity is what I'm really interested in. That's what we build these products for anyway. I want to be on the side of building what actually makes a difference to people. And I attempt to do this in multiple ways - my art, heart, work, and words.

Design is beauty without communicating what it's there for. We don't always need words to communicate, for sure. But, the RIGHT words can make things clearer and simply more engaging.

From a young age, I picked up multiple languages and dialects, mostly thanks to India's diversity reflected in my own family. But, my brain thinks and associates with the world with words, so I have always paid attention to what books say, advertisement hoardings say, sports commentators say, podcasters. **I am simply not the type to tune out words, I tune in. They're my frequency.**

It's sad that words have become excessive today to the point where we all just glaze over some of them and rely on skimming over reading. But thank god for content design, because we can actually help products communicate to users in the exact way needed!

While people may assume content design can be replaced by AI, I have learnt along the way that content design is less about the words and more about understanding context and the emotions of users.
