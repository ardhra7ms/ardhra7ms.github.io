I (much like other content designers) have been told to make something sound or seem better. While that's flattering from the idea that we may be better writers, that's not the purpose of our roles. We're here to build better products, and content is a lens to look at that process from.

**Content's unique value is the coherence it brings to everything in the product.** The words on the screen help users connect the dots about what is going on, what they need to do, and what'll happen as a result.

So, content design can more than enhance products. Good content strategy helps crystallise the product itself and is a great way for other functions - interaction design, visual design, UX research, CXE, Engineering, and PM - to all come together and agree to use one common language for the product that benefits the user to make the most of the values of the product.
