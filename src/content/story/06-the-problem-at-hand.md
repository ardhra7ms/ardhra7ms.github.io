**The best kind of problem is the one at hand.** I feel most inspired when I'm trying to solve a problem that has impact based on my life or work at that point in time, especially based on what I find missing and how I may be positioned to solve it even if it's not something I'm assigned to do. The contextual guidance tool and the compete study I'm working on currently are good examples of this because I identified gaps in where our work could raise our product content to be consumer grade level.

**I believe that as language and people's perspectives evolve, so should the products we use.** The product communicates to users through the content, so it's easily one of the ways to quickly appeal to and support them. I've always been a daughter of creativity and enjoyed exploring different avenues of art and pushing the limits of what I think I can or should do. If you glance through my work and projects, I believe that'll be evident. :)
